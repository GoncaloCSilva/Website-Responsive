Kid Cudi

Kid Cudi

Scott Ramon Seguro Mescudi (born January 30, 1984), better known by his stage name Kid Cudi, is an American rapper, singer, songwriter, record producer, and actor. He has been widely recognized as an influence on several contemporary hip hop and alternative acts. His lyrics are often autobiographical and describe his childhood hardships of depression, loneliness and alienation, his struggle with alcohol and drugs into adulthood, and themes of spirituality, heartbreak, dissipation and celebration.

Man on the Moon III: The Chosen is the seventh studio album by American musician Kid Cudi, released on December 11, 2020 by Republic Records. It is the final installment of Cudi's Man on the Moon trilogy of albums. Thematically, Man on the Moon III is a concept album that finds Cudi in an internal struggle against his evil alter ego, Mr. Rager, in hopes to win back his peace and happiness. As with the previous two installments in the trilogy, the album is divided into separate acts.


MAN ON THE MOON III TRACKLIST


Beautiful Trip

A short instrumental intro to the long-awaited third chapter of Cudi's Man on the Moon trilogy. This intro cleverly interpolates sounds from the opening of the first album in the series while preparing listeners for another "emotional moon landing."


Tequilla Shots

Kid Cudi embarks on the final chapter of the Man on the Moon trilogy by reflecting on past struggles and ongoing internal wars. Cudi depicts imagery of a storm stirring up within him, but he can't back down from the fight, despite his fears of crashing. He also mimics Common's narration as a callback to the first Man on the Moon.


Another Day

Kid Cudi raps about the adventures of Mr. Rager, but at the end of the track he seems to awaken from a night of partying with a desire to make a change in his life. He closes the song with a reference to his past life as Mr. Rager, hinting that he is taking his life in a different direction.


She Knows This

“She Knows This” revisits the theme of Cudi's trippy, occasionally superfluous, sexual parties. A lover at heart, Cudi's focus on his female counterparts is reflected not only in the title of the song, but also repeated throughout the chorus and described in the verses.


Dive

As the final song of Act I: Return 2 Madness, “Dive” continues the act's theme of alcohol-filled partying, followed by growing feelings of unease. Production is wielded by Dot Da Genius, but features production from others including Anthony Kilhoffer, Aaron Bow, Teddy Walton and Kevin Parker (aka Tame Impala). Cudi and Kilhoffer know each other through their affiliation with Kanye's G.O.O.D. Music record label; and Bow and Walton are often found working together on tracks.


Damaged

“Damaged” is the first track of Act II: The Rager, The Menace, and marks the start of Cudi's spiraling out of control in the plot of the album. The fast-paced life of his tour life, partying and sexual escapades has pushed Cudi into a damaged state of mind.


Heaven On Earth

As the second song of Act II: The Rager, The Menace, “Heaven on Earth” portrays Cudi riding high on his rockstar lifestyle. While experiencing the greater things in life and searching for “heaven on Earth”, he is guided by angels in his encounters the devilish behaviors. With its unwavering bassline and Cudi's uncharacteristic ad-libs, the track evokes violent undertones and Cudi seems to be out for blood.


Show Out

“Show Out” is a track of triumph and vigor, which combines related genres of drill and hip hop. Cudi flows in a never-before seen trio alongside the posthumous Brooklyn rapper Pop Smoke and London rapper Skepta. Produced by Cudi's OG team of Dot Da Genius and Plain Pat (as well as Heavy Mellow and Gravez), Cudi returns to his roots both vocally and instrumentally.


Mr. Solo Dolo III

On “Mr. Solo Dolo III,” Cudi delves into his addiction and struggles stemming from his isolation. Throughout his career, Cudi has been well-known as a pioneer of openness and honesty related to struggles with mental health in hip-hop, a genre that has typically bolstered the male image of high levels of confidence and no invulnerabilities to remark.


Sad People

On “Sad People,” Kid Cudi details the thoughts that plague him at night and his search for inner peace. He illustrates the extreme highs that he experiences, and how he often finds himself depressed afterward. Despite his sadness, he continues to push his limits, even when he knows it may not be the best for his mental health.


Elsie's Baby Boy (Flashback)

“Elsie's Baby Boy (Flashback)” has Cudi revisiting his childhood and singing about his relationship with his mother Elsie. He speaks on how hard she worked to keep their family together despite the lack of a traditional father figure, and how his father's death affected him.


Sept. 16th

“Sept. 16th” is the birthday of Cudi's girlfriend at the time of the MOTMIII release, Raquel “Rocky” Deriane. She appears to be the inspiration for this love-fueled ballad. FINNEAS hops on the track for production, but he is known as the primary producer for his sister Billie Eilish, and also is listed as a writer and producer for the song.


The Void

On “The Void,” Cudi details his struggles with mental health, describing how he will do anything to avoid succumbing to depression. He also takes a moment to thank his fans for supporting him through the years and sticking along for the ride.


Lovin'Me

For the emotive “Lovin' Me,” Cudi enlists indie artist Phoebe Bridgers to deliver an anthem on the importance of self-love in finding happiness, with the pair singing verses in a touching duet that marks the conclusion of the album's third act.

The Pale Moonlight

The song “The Pale Moonlight” is the first song in Act IV: Powers from Man on the Moon III: The Chosen. On this song Kid Cudi demonstrates his ability to ride on the beat effortlessly and shows his carefree spirit through the lyrics of this song.

Rockstar Knights

“Rockstar Knights” is a trippy, forward-thinking trap cut reminiscent of Cudi's more braggadocious material. Trippie touches on his potential mortality while flexing in typical fashion, while Cudi splits his verses between “King Wizard”-style braggadocio and introspection.


4 da Kidz

On the penultimate song to the third chapter in the Man on the Moon trilogy, Kid Cudi communicates a simple message to the kids—you're not alone. Cudi dedicates the song to fans who have struggled like he has, which is a theme he's been trying to put front and center his whole career. Embarking on his Man on the Moon journey, he declared his mission statement on 2009's “Soundtrack 2 My Life”: "I try and think about myself as a sacrifice Just to show the kids they ain't the only ones who up at night"


Lord I Know

“Lord I Know” serves as the closer to both the album and the Man on the Moon trilogy. On the track Cudi ruminates about the trials and tribulations he has faced, and understanding the weight of the message of perseverance he has championed throughout his career on tracks like “Pursuit of Happiness” and “Reborn” It finds Cudi finally content with life and the path that he is on. His daughter Vada closes the album with a short message as well.
